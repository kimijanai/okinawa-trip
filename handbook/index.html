Beaches, Islands & Attractions

Churaumi Aquarium

Churaumi Aquarium - Massive tank with whale sharks and manta rays

Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium is one of Japan's largest and most impressive aquariums, located within the Ocean Expo Park in Motobu. The highlight is the enormous Kuroshio Tank, which houses whale sharks, manta rays, and thousands of other fish in a mesmerizing display.

Highlights: Kuroshio Tank (one of the largest aquarium tanks in the world), dolphin shows, touch pools, coral reef exhibits, deep sea creatures.

Facilities: Restaurants, cafés, gift shops, nursing rooms, stroller rentals, wheelchair access.

Best for toddlers: The colorful fish displays, touch pools where children can interact with starfish and other marine life, and the impressive size of the whale sharks that capture even a young child's attention.

Kouri Island & Beach

Kouri Beach with heart-shaped rocks and crystal clear waters

Kouri Island is connected to Yagaji Island by the 2km-long Kouri Bridge, offering spectacular ocean views during the drive. The beach is known for its white sand, crystal clear waters, and famous heart-shaped rocks. The island has a relaxed atmosphere with several cafes offering ocean views.

Highlights: Heart-shaped rock formations, shallow waters ideal for toddlers, beautiful views of the Kouri Bridge.

Facilities: Restrooms, showers, nearby cafes and restaurants.

Best for toddlers: Several shallow, protected areas perfect for water play.

Miibaru Beach

Miibaru Beach with white sand and gentle slope into the water

Located in southern Okinawa, Miibaru Beach is known for its pristine white sand and crystal clear waters. The beach has a gentle slope into the water, making it perfect for families with young children. The surrounding area is less developed, offering a more natural beach experience.

Highlights: White sand, clear waters, gentle slope, natural setting away from crowds.

Facilities: Basic restrooms, some shade from trees, limited food options nearby.

Best for toddlers: The gentle slope into the water creates a large shallow area perfect for safe play.

Okinawa World

Gyokusendo Cave at Okinawa World with impressive limestone formations

Okinawa World is a cultural theme park showcasing Okinawan traditions and natural wonders. The highlight is Gyokusendo Cave, an 850-meter limestone cave with spectacular stalactites and stalagmites. The park also features traditional Okinawan crafts, snake shows, and cultural performances.

Highlights: Gyokusendo Cave, traditional craft demonstrations, Habu snake show, Eisa dance performances.

Facilities: Restaurants, gift shops, restrooms with changing tables, stroller-accessible paths (except in some cave areas).

Best for toddlers: The cave maintains a constant temperature and is naturally sheltered from weather, making it comfortable for families. The colorful cultural performances also captivate young children.

Fukushu-en Garden

Fukushu-en Garden with traditional Chinese architecture and peaceful ponds

Fukushu-en is a beautiful Chinese-style garden in Naha, created as a symbol of friendship between Naha and its sister city Fuzhou in China. The garden features traditional Chinese architecture, peaceful ponds with koi fish, stone bridges, and lush vegetation, creating a tranquil oasis in the city.

Highlights: Traditional Chinese pavilions, koi ponds, stone bridges, seasonal flower displays.

Facilities: Restrooms, some covered areas, tea house.

Best for toddlers: Spacious pathways for strollers, colorful koi fish that fascinate young children, and a peaceful environment for a relaxed visit.

Tsuboya Pottery Street

Tsuboya Pottery Street with traditional ceramic shops and demonstrations

Tsuboya Pottery Street is the traditional ceramics district of Naha, where you can explore numerous pottery shops, studios, and a small museum. The area has been producing Okinawan pottery for centuries and offers a glimpse into this important cultural tradition.

Highlights: Traditional pottery shops, Tsuboya Pottery Museum, artisan demonstrations, unique souvenirs.

Facilities: Small cafes, restrooms at the museum, narrow but navigable streets.

Best for toddlers: Colorful ceramic displays, watching potters at work, and a relaxed atmosphere where children can explore at their own pace.

Okinawan Cuisine & Restaurant Recommendations

Must-Try Okinawan Dishes

Goya Champuru - Okinawa's signature stir-fry with bitter melon, tofu, eggs, and pork

Okinawa Soba - Wheat noodles in pork-based broth with stewed pork belly

  • Goya Champuru (ゴーヤーチャンプルー) - Okinawa's signature stir-fry dish made with bitter melon, tofu, eggs, and pork. The word "champuru" means "mix" in the Okinawan dialect.
  • Okinawa Soba (沖縄そば) - Unlike mainland Japanese soba, Okinawan soba uses wheat noodles in a pork-based broth, topped with stewed pork belly (rafute), fish cake, and green onions.
  • Rafute (ラフテー) - Tender pork belly simmered in soy sauce, brown sugar, and awamori (Okinawan rice liquor). This melt-in-your-mouth dish is a staple at Okinawan celebrations.
  • Taco Rice (タコライス) - A fusion dish created in the 1960s near American military bases. It combines taco fillings (seasoned ground beef, lettuce, tomato, cheese) served over rice instead of a taco shell.
  • Umibudo (海ぶどう) - Known as "sea grapes" or "green caviar," these are small, edible seaweed that pop in your mouth like caviar. Usually served with ponzu sauce.

Toddler-Friendly Okinawan Foods

Kid-friendly Okinawan dishes including soft Rafute (pork belly) and Jimami Tofu

These dishes are generally suitable for a 1-year-old:

  • Okinawa Soba - The soft noodles and tender pork can be cut into small pieces for toddlers.
  • Rafute - The melt-in-your-mouth pork belly is easy for young children to eat.
  • Jimami Tofu (ジーマミー豆腐) - Peanut tofu with a soft texture and slightly sweet taste.
  • Steamed Rice - A safe staple available at most restaurants.
  • Tropical Fruits - Okinawa is known for delicious mangoes, pineapples, and other fruits that can be cut into small pieces.

Note: Always check for potential allergens, especially with peanut-based dishes like Jimami Tofu.

Okinawa Weather Information

Weather Overview for Your Trip (May 30-June 2)

Okinawa's subtropical climate with blue skies and occasional rain showers

Your trip falls at the beginning of Okinawa's rainy season, but this doesn't mean constant rain. Here's what to expect:

Rainfall Late May to Late June is rainy season (Tsuyu)
Average 12-16 rainy days per month
Often short, intense showers rather than all-day rain

Typical Daily Pattern

  • Mornings often start clear and sunny
  • Afternoon showers are common, sometimes intense but usually brief
  • Evenings may clear up again
  • Occasional full sunny days between rain periods

Packing Recommendations

Essential Items

  • Light, breathable clothing (cotton, linen)
  • Light rain jacket or poncho (for adults and child)
  • Compact umbrella
  • Waterproof phone case/bag
  • Quick-dry clothing
  • Sun protection (high SPF sunscreen, hats, sunglasses)
  • Insect repellent (humidity brings mosquitoes)

For Beach Days

  • UV-protective swimwear
  • Water shoes (for rocky beaches)
  • Quick-dry towels
  • Waterproof beach bag

For Your Toddler

  • Light, breathable clothes with UV protection
  • Sun hat with neck protection
  • Waterproof stroller cover
  • Extra changes of clothes (humidity can make children uncomfortable)

Weather-Related Travel Tips

Planning Your Days

  • Check daily weather forecasts each morning
  • Plan indoor activities for afternoons when showers are more likely
  • Take advantage of clear mornings for outdoor activities
  • Have your rainy day alternatives ready (as provided in your itinerary)

Driving in Rain

  • Allow extra travel time during rainy conditions
  • Be cautious on mountain roads which can become slippery
  • Turn headlights on during rain (required by law in Japan)
  • Maintain greater following distance

Health Considerations

  • Stay hydrated (the humid climate can cause dehydration even when it doesn't feel hot)
  • Take breaks in air-conditioned spaces during particularly humid days
  • Watch for signs of heat exhaustion, even on cloudy days
  • Apply sunscreen regularly, even when cloudy (UV rays penetrate cloud cover)

Typhoon Information

While early June is not peak typhoon season (which typically runs from July to October), occasional early typhoons can occur:

  • Typhoon forecasts are very reliable and provided 3-5 days in advance
  • Major hotels and tourist facilities are well-prepared for typhoon situations
  • If a typhoon warning is issued, follow all local guidance and shelter advisories
  • Consider travel insurance that covers weather-related disruptions

Weather Resources

  • Japan Meteorological Agency: www.jma.go.jp/jma/indexe.html (English available)
  • Okinawa Weather App: "Okinawa Weather" available on iOS and Android
  • Emergency Weather Alerts: Will be sent to all mobile phones in the area (even foreign phones)
