General Rainy Day Tips

  • Always carry:
    • Compact umbrellas for the whole family
    • Rain cover for stroller
    • Extra change of clothes for the toddler
    • Waterproof bag for wet items
  • Transportation:
    • Allow extra travel time as traffic can be heavier during rain
    • Use covered parking areas where available
    • Keep a towel in the car to dry off before getting in
  • Booking:
    • Many indoor attractions get busier during rainy days
    • Consider making reservations for restaurants and activities when possible
    • Check opening hours as some attractions may have reduced hours during inclement weather
  • Health Considerations:
    • Rainy season in Okinawa can be humid - ensure the baby stays comfortable
    • Air-conditioned indoor venues provide relief from humidity
    • Stay hydrated even when it's not hot outside

Key Attractions

Kouri Beach

Image: Kouri Beach with crystal clear waters and heart-shaped rocks

Located at Kouri Island, connected by the 2 km-long Kouri Bridge. Features translucent water, marine leisure activities, and boutique cafes and eateries within walking distance. The beach is known for its fine white sand and crystal clear waters, making it perfect for families with young children.

Highlights: Heart-shaped rock formations, shallow waters ideal for toddlers, beautiful views of the Kouri Bridge.

Facilities: Restrooms, showers, nearby cafes and restaurants.

Best time to visit: Morning to early afternoon when the sun isn't too strong.

Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium

Image: Churaumi Aquarium's massive Kuroshio Sea tank with whale sharks

One of the largest aquariums in the world, featuring the massive Kuroshio Sea tank where whale sharks and manta rays swim. The aquarium has numerous exhibits showcasing Okinawa's diverse marine life, from colorful coral reefs to deep-sea creatures.

Highlights: Kuroshio Sea tank, dolphin shows, coral reef exhibits, touch pools, outdoor playground.

Facilities: Restaurants, gift shops, nursing rooms, stroller rentals, rest areas.

Best for toddlers: The colorful fish displays, touch pools, and outdoor playground areas.

Nago Pineapple Park

Image: Pineapple-shaped carts at Nago Pineapple Park

A theme park dedicated to pineapples, where visitors can ride pineapple-shaped carts through pineapple fields and tropical gardens. The park offers various pineapple products to taste and purchase.

Highlights: Pineapple cart ride, tropical fruit tasting, Dinosaur Adventure area, gift shop with unique pineapple products.

Facilities: Restrooms, cafe, gift shop, parking.

Best for toddlers: The colorful cart ride and dinosaur displays.

Southeast Botanical Gardens

Image: Lush tropical plants and water features at Southeast Botanical Gardens

A sprawling botanical garden featuring tropical and subtropical plants from around the world. The garden is divided into different themed areas, including a water garden, fruit garden, and palm garden.

Highlights: Water lily pond, tropical fruit trees, palm collection, seasonal flower displays.

Facilities: Cafe, gift shop, restrooms, stroller-friendly paths.

Best for toddlers: Open spaces to explore, water features, and shaded areas for rest.

Blue Seal Ice Park

Image: Ice cream making experience at Blue Seal Ice Park

A popular ice cream factory where visitors can learn about ice cream production and create their own original ice cream flavors. Blue Seal is an iconic Okinawan ice cream brand with a history dating back to 1948.

Highlights: Ice cream making experience, factory tour, tasting various flavors.

Facilities: Ice cream shop, cafe, gift shop, restrooms.

Best for toddlers: Colorful environment and, of course, ice cream tasting!

Kokusai Street Shopping District

Image: Vibrant shops and restaurants along Kokusai Street

The main shopping street in Naha City, stretching about 1.6 km with approximately 600 stores and businesses. The street offers everything from souvenir shops to restaurants, clothing stores, and local craft shops.

Highlights: Souvenir shopping, local cuisine, connected covered shopping arcades including Makishi Public Market.

Facilities: Numerous restaurants, shops, restrooms, some areas with seating.

Best for toddlers: The covered shopping arcades provide shelter from sun or rain, and there are many colorful displays to keep little ones entertained.

Travel Tips for Families with Toddlers

Before You Go

  • Packing Essentials:
    • Lightweight, breathable clothing (Okinawa is subtropical)
    • Sun protection (hats, sunscreen, UV-protective clothing)
    • Baby carrier/sling (often more practical than strollers in some areas)
    • Compact stroller for urban areas and shopping malls
    • Swim diapers and regular diapers for beach days
    • Portable changing pad
    • Familiar snacks and comfort items from home

Transportation

  • Car Rental: Highly recommended for families with toddlers. Allows flexibility with nap schedules and carrying baby supplies.
  • Car Seats: Rental companies provide child seats, but confirm availability when booking.
  • Driving: Keep drives under 1.5 hours when possible to accommodate toddler's comfort.
  • Parking: Most major attractions have dedicated parking lots (fees may apply).

Baby Supplies in Okinawa

  • Diapers: Japanese brands like Merries, Moony, and Goo.n are excellent quality and widely available at drugstores and supermarkets.
  • Baby Food: Available at supermarkets and drugstores. Look for brands like Kewpie and Wakodo.
  • Formula: Japanese formula brands are available, but if your baby is particular, bring enough from home.
  • Pharmacies: Drugstores like Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Daikoku Drug, and Cosmos carry baby essentials.

Beach Tips with Toddlers

  • Timing: Visit beaches in the morning when temperatures are milder.
  • Shade: Bring or rent a beach umbrella or tent for sun protection.
  • Water Safety: Many beaches have gentle slopes and calm waters, but always supervise closely.
  • Beach Facilities: Beaches like Emerald Beach and Zanpa Beach have restrooms and changing facilities.
  • Footwear: Bring water shoes for rocky areas and hot sand.

Dining with Toddlers

  • High Chairs: Available at most family restaurants but not guaranteed at smaller establishments.
  • Kid-Friendly Restaurants:
    • Family restaurant chains like Coco's and Joyfull are well-equipped for children
    • Food courts in shopping malls offer variety and casual atmosphere
    • Sam's by the Sea in Awase is family-friendly with seafood options
    • Fisherman's Wharf offers ocean views and child-friendly menu items
  • Meal Timing: Consider eating earlier than typical Japanese dinner times (before 6:30 PM).
  • Snacks: Carry familiar snacks for your toddler to avoid hunger meltdowns during sightseeing.

Health and Safety

  • Sun Protection: Okinawa's UV index is high year-round. Apply sunscreen frequently and keep babies in shade when possible.
  • Hydration: Carry water bottles and offer drinks frequently, especially in warmer months.
  • Medical Facilities:
    • Heartlife Hospital (English-speaking staff): 098-895-3255
    • Adventist Medical Center (English-speaking staff): 098-868-9966
    • Emergency number: 119
  • First Aid Kit: Include baby-specific items like infant fever reducer, diaper rash cream, and band-aids.

Managing Jet Lag with Toddlers

  • Gradual Adjustment: Start adjusting sleep schedules a few days before departure.
  • Outdoor Time: Spend time outdoors during daylight hours to help reset circadian rhythms.
  • Flexible Schedule: Plan lighter activities for the first 1-2 days to allow for adjustment.
  • Maintain Some Routines: Keep some elements of your toddler's bedtime routine consistent.
  • Nap Strategy: For Day 1, a slightly longer afternoon nap at the hotel can help with the transition.
